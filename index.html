Florence Nightingale

"The Lady with the Lamp"

Life story of Florence Nightingale :

  • 1820 - Born in Villa Colombia
  • 1837 - Received a divine calling from God
  • 1851- Trained as a nurse in Kaiserwerth, Germany at Pastor Theodore Fliedner's Hospital and school for Lutheran deaconesses.
  • 1853- Went for additional training in Paris with sisters of Mercy. After returning she took up a position as superintendent for London's Establishment of Gentlewomen during illness.
  • 1854-1856- On November 4, 1854 Nightingale and 38 nurses arrived in Scutari, location of the British camp outside Constantinople.
  • 1857-Florence returned as a heroine. She spent the next few years at the Royal Commission investigating health in the British Army.
  • 1859- Florence Nightingale’s book Notes on Nursing: What it is, and What it is Not was published. Based on knowledge acquired at school in Kaiserswerth and while nursing the sick during the Crimean War, Notes on Nursing provides a simple but practical discussion of good patient care, along with helpful hints.
  • 1860-During the war a public subscription fund was set up for Florence Nightingale to continue her education of nurses in England, and the Nightingale Training School at St. Thomas’ Hospital opened.
  • 1870- Trained the first American nurse named Linda and enabled her to return to America with adequate training.
  • 1882- Several nurses trained under Nightingale became matrons at several leading hospitals in London.
  • 1883- Royal Red Cross award was awarded by Queen Victoria.
  • 1904- Nightingale was appointed as a Lady of Grace of the Order at St.John's hospital.
  • 1907- She became the first woman to be awarded the Order of Merit. In the following year she was given the Honorary Freedom of the City of London..
  • 1910- died at the age of 90.
